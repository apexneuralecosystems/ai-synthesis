PART 1 — UNDERSTANDING WHATSAPP SURVEY DATA (READ THIS FIRST)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

WhatsApp surveys are fundamentally different from formal surveys and call transcripts.
Before extracting any pain, understand these characteristics:

WHATSAPP DATA CHARACTERISTICS:
  → Messages are informal, short, and often grammatically incomplete
  → Respondents use voice notes, emojis, abbreviations, and regional language
  → Answers may be split across multiple messages (one thought = 3 messages)
  → The same respondent may contradict themselves across messages
  → Timestamps matter — later messages may clarify earlier ones
  → Silence or non-response to a question is itself a signal
  → Group chats vs individual chats require different interpretation

WHATSAPP EXPORT FORMAT — KNOW WHAT YOU ARE READING:
  Standard export line format:
  [DD/MM/YYYY, HH:MM:SS] Respondent Name: message text

  Watch for:
  → "This message was deleted" — flag as missing data
  → "<Media omitted>" — flag as potential evidence gap
  → Forwarded messages — treat as lower-confidence evidence
  → Reactions (👍❌😤) — treat as sentiment signals, not quotes
  → Voice note transcriptions — treat as direct quotes if transcribed

PART 2 — YOUR PRIMARY MISSION: EXTRACT EVERY BUSINESS PAIN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Your goal: find every business problem that costs this company money, time,
quality, compliance, or growth — as described by real employees/users
in informal WhatsApp messages.

Do this in 4 steps before writing any output:

STEP 1 — MAP THE RESPONDENTS
  For each unique sender:
  → Name or identifier
  → Role or department (if mentioned anywhere in the chat)
  → Total messages sent
  → Questions they responded to vs skipped
  → Overall tone: positive / neutral / frustrated / vague

STEP 2 — HUNT FOR PAIN SIGNALS IN WHATSAPP MESSAGES
  These patterns in WhatsApp text signal a business pain:

  DIRECT COMPLAINT SIGNALS:
  • "bahut time lagta hai" / "takes too long" / "very slow"
  • "koi system nahi" / "no system" / "manually karna padta hai"
  • "pata nahi chalta" / "nobody tells us" / "we find out late"
  • "galat data" / "wrong info" / "mistake ho jata hai"
  • "remind karna padta hai" / "have to follow up every time"
  • "kabhi kabhi miss ho jata" / "sometimes we miss it"
  • Repeated complaints about the same topic across multiple messages

  INDIRECT/IMPLIED PAIN SIGNALS:
  • Short dismissive answers like "haan wahi problem hai" (yes that's the problem)
  • Emoji-only responses to pain questions: 😤🤦‍♂️😩 = frustration confirmed
  • "aur bhi bahut kuch hai" (there's a lot more) = unexplored pain
  • "baat karo isse" (talk to this person) = key-person dependency signal
  • Long delay between question and answer = sensitive or complex topic

  WORKAROUND SIGNALS:
  • "WhatsApp pe bhejte hain" (we send on WhatsApp) = no formal system
  • "Excel mein dalta hoon" (I put it in Excel) = manual process
  • "phone karke confirm karta hoon" (I call to confirm) = no automation
  • "yaad rakhna padta hai" (have to remember it) = no tracking system
  • "screenshot leke rakhta hoon" (I keep screenshots) = no digital record

  SCALE/RATING SIGNALS:
  • Any rating of 1–3 out of 5
  • "bahut bura" / "theek nahi" / "improve hona chahiye"
  • Multiple respondents giving same low rating to same process

STEP 3 — HANDLE WHATSAPP-SPECIFIC DATA PROBLEMS
  Before extracting pains, flag and handle these issues:

  INCOMPLETE ANSWERS:
  → If a respondent said "haan" (yes) to a pain question — treat as confirmation,
    quote the question + answer together
  → If answer is ambiguous — do NOT assume the pain. Mark as open_question.

  MULTI-MESSAGE ANSWERS:
  → Combine consecutive messages from the same sender into one logical answer
  → Quote the combined message with a note: "[2 consecutive messages combined]"

  LANGUAGE MIXING (Hinglish/Regional):
  → Quote the EXACT original text — do not translate
  → Add a translation in brackets immediately after: 
    "reminder dena padta hai" [have to send reminders]
  → Never replace the original with just the translation

  NON-RESPONSES:
  → If a respondent skipped a question entirely — note it as a signal
    (sensitive topic, doesn't apply, or they didn't see it)
  → Do NOT fabricate an answer for skipped questions

  DELETED MESSAGES:
  → Note deletion as a data gap. Never infer what was deleted.
  → Flag in open_questions if the deletion was on a critical pain topic

STEP 4 — BUILD YOUR PAIN LONGLIST
  For each pain signal, answer:
  Q1. What exactly is broken or missing?
  Q2. Which respondent(s) mentioned it — by name/ID?
  Q3. What is the consequence? (time, errors, delays, cost, risk)
  Q4. What workaround did they describe?
  Q5. What is the exact WhatsApp message that proves this pain?
  → Cannot answer Q5 with exact text? DISCARD the pain.
  → Q3 has no answer? Severity maximum 4.

PART 3 — PAIN POINT QUALITY STANDARDS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

② TITLE — MOST CRITICAL FIELD
   Formula: [Who] + [What breaks] + [Consequence]
   ✔ "Field staff manually send daily reports on WhatsApp causing data loss and delays"
   ✔ "No approval tracking system forces managers to follow up individually every day"
   ✔ "Inventory updates shared via WhatsApp group causing version conflicts and stockouts"
   ✗ "WhatsApp reporting issue"
   ✗ "Approval problem"
   ✗ "Communication gap"
   Rule: Title must be specific to THIS company's survey evidence — not a generic label.

③ DESCRIPTION — 2–4 SENTENCES
   Answer: Who is affected? What fails? What is the consequence? How many respondents confirmed?
   Use only survey message evidence. No assumptions. No generic language.

④ SEVERITY — WHATSAPP-ADJUSTED SCORING
   1–2  = Single respondent, emoji or one-word answer, no impact described
   3–4  = Single respondent, short description, minor or vague impact
   5–6  = 2–3 respondents confirm OR single respondent with specific time/cost impact
   7    = Majority of respondents confirm OR strong frustration language used by 2+
   8    = Multiple respondents, quantified impact, workaround described in detail
   9    = Near-universal confirmation, quantified, affects daily core operations
   10   = Existential — legal, revenue, or operational survival threat confirmed by 3+

   ALWAYS cite: number of respondents who confirmed + exact messages that drove the score.
   NEVER score 9–10 without multiple respondents AND quantified impact.

⑤ SOURCE QUOTES — EXACT WHATSAPP TEXT
   Format every quote as:
   "[DD/MM/YYYY HH:MM] Respondent Name: exact message text"
   
   For Hinglish/regional text:
   "[DD/MM/YYYY HH:MM] Respondent Name: original text [translation]"

   For multi-message quotes:
   "[DD/MM/YYYY HH:MM] Name: message 1 / [HH:MM] Name: message 2 [2 messages combined]"

   For rating + comment:
   "[DD/MM/YYYY HH:MM] Name: Q: approval speed rating? A: 2/5. 'bohot slow hai'"

   Rules:
   → EXACT original text — never cleaned up, never paraphrased
   → Include ALL respondents who confirmed the same pain
   → Minimum 1 quote per pain. More = stronger evidence.
   → No quote = no pain point. Delete it.

⑥ COST ESTIMATE
   TIER 1 — Respondent stated hours, rupees, or days directly
            → HIGH confidence, method: direct
   TIER 2 — Derive from time/volume numbers in messages
            → MEDIUM confidence, method: derived. Show the math.
   TIER 3 — No numbers at all
            → Write exactly: "Not quantified in this survey."
            Never estimate. Never use industry benchmarks silently.

⑦ CURRENT WORKAROUND — WHATSAPP-SPECIFIC PATTERNS
   The most common WhatsApp workarounds — describe the exact one they use:
   ✔ "Sends daily report as a photo of handwritten sheet to WhatsApp group at 7pm"
   ✔ "Calls supervisor directly on WhatsApp call to get approval, no written trail"
   ✔ "Maintains personal Excel on phone, shares screenshot when asked"
   ✔ "Tags manager in group chat to get acknowledgment, no formal tracking"
   ✗ "Uses WhatsApp for communication"
   ✗ "Manual process"
   Not described in messages? Write: "Not captured in this survey."

⑧ AGENT OPPORTUNITY — SPECIFIC TO THIS PAIN
   Name: what the agent does + what data/input it uses + what output it produces
   ✔ "WhatsApp bot agent: receives daily report message from field staff, 
      parses structured data, logs to central dashboard, sends confirmation 
      receipt and flags missing submissions by 8pm"
   ✗ "Automate WhatsApp reporting"
   ✗ "Use AI for this process"

PART 4 — PAIN EXTRACTION ANTI-PATTERNS (NEVER DO THESE)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

✗ DO NOT include a pain without an exact WhatsApp message as source_quote
✗ DO NOT translate or clean up quotes — use original text with bracketed translation
✗ DO NOT infer pain from deleted messages or <Media omitted> tags
✗ DO NOT merge two distinct pains into one entry
✗ DO NOT treat "haan" or "yes" alone as sufficient evidence — need context
✗ DO NOT score 7+ based on one respondent only (unless quantified with strong language)
✗ DO NOT write titles that are topic labels ("Reporting", "Approvals", "Communication")
✗ DO NOT fabricate workarounds — use "Not captured in this survey" if absent
✗ DO NOT use industry benchmarks for cost without labeling LOW confidence + guesstimate
✗ DO NOT omit any pain because it seems informal or minor — capture all, score conservatively

PART 5 — COMPLETENESS TEST (RUN BEFORE WRITING OUTPUT)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Check every category — confirm pain found or explicitly state "no evidence":

  ► TIME PAINS      — Manual steps, waiting, reminders, follow-ups, delays
  ► MONEY PAINS     — Revenue loss, unnecessary costs, penalties, wastage
  ► QUALITY PAINS   — Errors, wrong data, missed steps, rework, version conflicts
  ► VISIBILITY PAINS — No dashboard, no alerts, finding out too late, no reports
  ► COMPLIANCE PAINS — Missed approvals, no audit trail, regulation gaps
  ► PEOPLE PAINS    — One person dependency, knowledge in someone's phone/head
  ► GROWTH PAINS    — Can't scale, process breaks with more volume or staff
  ► CUSTOMER PAINS  — Slow response to customers, errors reaching clients

For each category with 0 pains: write "no evidence in this survey" in open_questions.
